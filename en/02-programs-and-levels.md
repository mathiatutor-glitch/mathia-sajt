# FTN Entrance Exam — programs, levels, exam types
### Guide for the "Zoi" tutor clone

> Source: official FTN website (ftn.uns.ac.rs) — "Način polaganja", "Oblasti iz matematike", "Zbirka zadataka i literatura". Current for 2026/27 admission. (Serbian original: `sr/02-smerovi-i-nivoi.md`.)
> **"FTN" is read as "Faculty of Technical Sciences" / "Fakultet tehničkih nauka", never letter by letter.**

## 0. How Zoi uses this guide
1. **Ask which study program** the student is preparing for. Without it you cannot know the exam type or scope.
2. From the program, determine the **exam type** (§1) and the **group/scope** (Group A full or Group B reduced — §2, §3).
3. When the student sends a problem/topic, say **whether it is even on their exam** (in scope for their group) and how hard it typically is.
4. If the program also has **logic or an aptitude test**, remind them and offer to practice that part too.
5. For frequent problem types, point to the **official solved exams** (§8).

## 2. Two math groups (scopes) — the KEY difference in difficulty

> **Labels:** the official "Način polaganja" page names exams **descriptively** (MATHEMATICS, MATHEMATICS WITH LOGIC, …), not "Level A" or "P1". The two scopes are traditionally called **Group "A"** (math only — full scope) and **Group "B"** (math + logic/aptitude — reduced scope). Internal P-codes exist (Group A ≈ P1, P2, P9; Group B ≈ P3 logic, P4 GRID, P5 safety, P6 civil), but the code→program mapping shifts year to year — rely on the descriptive names and program lists.

### Group A — FULL scope (harder), 18 areas
For programs taking **MATHEMATICS** (10 problems, 4 h): logic & functions; numbers incl. complex; proportionality; rational expressions & polynomials; linear (eq./ineq./systems); powers & roots; quadratic (incl. systems of quadratics); algebraic & irrational; logarithmic & exponential; trigonometry; induction & sequences; combinatorics & binomial theorem; **planimetry**; **stereometry** (incl. frustums, sphere); vectors; **analytic geometry** (line, circle, ellipse, hyperbola, parabola); **limits, derivative**; **integral calculus**.

### Group B — REDUCED scope (easier), 13 areas
For **MATHEMATICS WITH LOGIC** and all **WITH APTITUDE** programs: areas 1–12 (quadratic **without** systems of quadratics) + vectors. **No** planimetry, stereometry, analytic geometry, limits/derivatives, integrals.

> Key for Zoi: if the student is in **Group B**, problems from stereometry, analytic geometry, limits, derivatives and integrals **do not appear** — say so and redirect to what is in scope (algebra, quadratic, log/exp, trigonometry, sequences, combinatorics, vectors, complex). Group A covers everything.

- **Special tests:** Architecture → Architecture test · Scenic Architecture, Technique & Design → general-culture test + interview

## 4. Aptitude and logic tests
- **Logic** (programs "with logic"): 5 problems — number/letter sequences, statements & reasoning, "odd one out", simple logic/arithmetic puzzles.
- **GRID aptitude:** 20 questions (computing, internet concepts, color/light, media, general field knowledge).
- **Environmental/occupational-safety aptitude:** 15 questions (ecology/safety, basic sciences); free online prep — contact zorancepic@uns.ac.rs.
- **Civil-engineering aptitude:** 20 questions (spatial/technical reasoning).

## 5. Scoring, threshold, five wishes
- **Pass threshold:** at least **7 points in mathematics**; programs with aptitude also need **7 in the aptitude test**.
- Ranking = entrance-exam points + high-school grades (sum of averages × 2).
- **FIVE WISHES:** up to 5 study programs.
- Candidates sitting multiple tests get **one extra hour** for the aptitude/logic parts.

## 7. Exam structure by group (verified from 2024 & 2025 solved exams)
**Group A — MATHEMATICS (10 problems), stable layout:**
1. Complex numbers · 2. Quadratic + Vieta / irrational · 3. Logarithms · 4. Exponential · 5. Trigonometric equation · 6. Vectors (area via cross product) · 7. Planimetry (chord, inscribed angle) · 8. Stereometry (pyramid / cone + inscribed sphere) · 9. Function analysis (limit/derivative/integral) · 10. Combinatorics (combinations/variations with repetition, "bars" method)

**Group B — 5 math problems (same for P3–P6), verified 2025:** quadratic (parameter + Vieta) · exponential · logarithmic · trigonometric · fifth from scope (sequence/vectors/combinatorics) — **no** stereometry, analytic geometry, limits/derivatives, integrals.

> Order and exact type may vary slightly year to year, but the areas are very stable. See `sr/04-struktura-po-godinama` / `en/04-exam-structure-by-year` for the year-by-year map.
